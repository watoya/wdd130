In the summer of 1724, the hidden city of Eldermere was discovered deep within the uncharted forests of the New World. Thought to be nothing more than a legend whispered among sailors and scholars, the city was said to have been founded by a lost sect of explorers who vanished from European records nearly a century prior. When Captain Elias Grayson and his expedition stumbled upon its towering stone walls, they were met with a civilization untouched by time, speaking an archaic dialect of Old Norse mixed with indigenous tongues. The people of Eldermere, calling themselves the Varani, claimed descent from a fleet of Viking longships that had sailed westward in the 10th century, escaping the wars of their homeland in search of a paradise. Over generations, they had built a thriving society, blending Norse traditions with the rich knowledge of the native tribes they encountered. Eldermere’s greatest secret, however, was its fabled Heartstone—an enormous crystal at the center of the city, rumored to possess mystical properties.

History

Captain Grayson’s journals describe it as glowing with an inner light, pulsating like a living heart. Though he intended to return to Europe with evidence of his discovery, neither he nor his crew were ever seen again. The city of Eldermere vanished into myth once more, its existence debated by historians and treasure seekers alike. Some say the Varani erased all traces of the intruders, while others believe the Heartstone itself had a hand in their disappearance. To this day, no expedition has ever found Eldermere again, but scattered maps and cryptic carvings hint that the lost city, and its powerful secret, still lies hidden within the vast wilderness, waiting to be uncovered once more.
